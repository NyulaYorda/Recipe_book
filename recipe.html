Waakye Recipe

Ingredients

  • 5 ½ cups white rice
  • 2 cups black eye peas
  • 10-12 pieces of millet or waakye leaves
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 5-7 cups water

Instructions

  1. Wash the beans and soak them overnight in enough water to cover them by 2 inches. If you are pressed for time, soak for at least 30 minutes.
  2. The next day, drain the beans and transfer them to a large pot. Add the millet or waakye leaves, baking soda, and 3 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes, or until the beans are tender.
  3. Add the rice to the pot and stir to combine. Add 3 cups of water and salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through.
  4. Take out the waakye leaves and fluff the rice with a fork. Cover the pot and let it sit for 5 minutes.
  5. Serve the waakye hot with your favorite stew, fried fish, or other side dishes.

Tips

  • If you prefer your waakye very soft, add more water, about ½ - 1 cup.
  • You can also cook the beans and rice in a pressure cooker. This will save time and make the dish more flavorful.
  • To make a tomato stew, heat some oil in a pot over medium heat. Add some chopped onions and cook until softened. Add some chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, and your favorite spices. Cook until the stew is thickened.
